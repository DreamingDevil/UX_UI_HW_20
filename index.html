Enhancing USDA Website for Seamless User Experience



We tackled the challenge of creating a Responsive Web Redesign for the United States Department of Agriculture's primary website, focusing on delivering a seamless user experience.

Our primary goal was to enhance the information architecture across various devices and ensure accessibility on the homepage.

User Insights

Proto-Personas, Research Insights, and User Personas



Proto-Persona: Meet Stoic Steve

Stoic Steve, representing farmers, guided our user-centric approach. By studying Steve's needs, we ensured that the redesign catered to practical agricultural requirements.


Research Insights

In just 5 days, we gathered insights from various users, identifying pain points and challenges in navigating the current site. Our approach combined user interviews with extensive secondary research.

Defining User-Centric Challenges

User Insights, Problem Statement, and Hypotheses



User-Centric Insights

Our research highlighted that users, like Sophisticated Steve, struggle to navigate the current site efficiently, leading to frustration and a potential erosion of trust. They seek quick access to vital agricultural information.


Problem Statement

The existing website's organizational structure doesn't align with user mental models. This disconnect hinders users from quickly finding and accessing essential resources, negatively impacting their experience.


Hypotheses for Enhancement

We hypothesize that by optimizing menu structures and strategically categorizing content, we can significantly improve users' ability to locate information, reducing the time and effort required.

Crafting User-Focused Solutions

Information Architecture, Wireframing, and Testing



Revamped Information Architecture

Our approach involved a user-centric information architecture, reimagining the menu structure. We aimed to categorize content based on user needs, simplifying navigation and improving content discoverability.


Iterative Wireframing and Testing

We rapidly prototyped various designs, rigorously testing each iteration with real users. Insights from testing informed our decisions, leading to a refined and effective design solution.

Design System Integration

We seamlessly integrated our design within the USDA's brand identity, maintaining visual consistency. Our design choices emphasized legibility, contrast, and accessibility to ensure a positive user experience.


Continual Refinement

With a user-centered design approach, we iterated based on feedback, focusing on improving usability and minimizing any friction points that arose during user testing.

User-Centric High-Fidelity Prototypes

Final Iterations

Empowering Users

Our final prototypes reflect the culmination of user-centric design principles. The sitemap and menus have been refined to ensure users can efficiently access critical resources with minimal effort.


Optimized Homepage

We carefully considered the balance of content to minimize scrolling while maximizing content visibility. Dynamic design elements were chosen based on their efficacy, enhancing user engagement.

Reflections and Valuable Lessons

Key Insights



User-Centric Iteration

Throughout this project, we've learned that a relentless focus on user needs is the bedrock of successful design. Agile iteration and user testing have consistently guided us toward solutions that truly resonate with users.


Harmony of Simplicity

The power of simplicity cannot be overstated. Striking the right balance between intuitive design and visual elegance is key to creating an environment where users can engage without distraction.
